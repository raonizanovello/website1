About

Meet your coach.

I'm Ronnie — a CSCS-certified strength and conditioning coach working with a small roster of private clients in San Francisco. I don't guess at movement. I assess it, track it, and build programs that hold up under real schedules.

Most of my clients are senior professionals in tech and health — people managing companies, teams, or both. They don't have time for perfect conditions. So we build training that survives travel weeks, back-to-back quarters, and 60-hour pushes — and still drives measurable progress.

I also compete in powerlifting. The standard I coach is the standard I train under.

What Working With Me Looks Like

This is coaching built to survive real life.

This isn't a gym membership with a name attached. Every engagement starts with a full movement assessment, and programming is built from what I find — not from a template.

Between sessions, you have direct access to me for adjustments, questions, and check-ins. When your schedule shifts, your programming shifts with it. The goal is a training system that runs in the background of your life and keeps compounding whether the week is clean or chaotic.

I keep my roster intentionally small. If I'm at capacity, I'll let you know and put you on the list.

Assessment-first

We start with how you move now, where you're restricted, and what is actually worth changing first.

Direct access between sessions

You are not waiting a week for programming to catch up to travel, fatigue, or a schedule shift.

Programming that compounds

The aim is consistency under pressure, not perfect adherence under ideal conditions.

Intentionally small roster

I work with a limited number of people so feedback stays specific, response time stays tight, and programming never becomes generic.
