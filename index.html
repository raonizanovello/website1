Client Story

From chronic back pain to a 300 lb deadlift.

A senior engineering manager came to me with a decade-old back injury, 60-hour weeks, and a history of programs that collapsed within a month. He'd been told to "just be careful" with his back and had trained around it for years.

The movement screen told a different story: limited hip mobility was the root cause — not a structural problem, a training gap. We built a three-day program he could complete during lunch or in a hotel gym.

Six months later, his back pain went from chronic to occasional. His deadlift went from 185 to 305 lbs. He trained in 46 of 52 weeks — including two international work trips.

FAQ

Honest answers.

Do I need access to a specific gym?

No. Programs are designed around whatever you have access to — a full commercial gym, a home rack, hotel fitness centers, or a set of dumbbells. Gym access is mapped during the Schedule Mapping step. If your access changes, the program changes with it.

What if I travel frequently or my schedule is unpredictable?

That's the typical use case here. Every program includes travel-week and minimal-equipment variants. If you're on the road three out of four weeks, we build the primary program around that — the stable-schedule weeks become the exception, not the rule.

Is this remote or in-person coaching?

Both options are available. Online coaching is fully remote — programming delivered through an app, strategy calls twice a month, and direct messaging access in between. In-person coaching is available in the SF Bay Area and for select clients in Sacramento County. Many clients start online and add in-person sessions later.

What does a typical week actually look like?

Most clients train three days per week, 45–60 minutes per session. Sessions are structured around your schedule — not a fixed template. A standard week might be lower body, upper body, and a full-body day, with a travel alternative for each. See the Sample Training Week above for a representative breakdown.

How does the initial assessment work?

The assessment happens in your first session (after the consultation). It covers a movement screen covering mobility, stability, and motor control, plus strength baselines and a schedule mapping conversation. The full process takes 60–75 minutes. Your first written program is delivered within 48 hours.

What's the minimum commitment?

The initial commitment is 3 months — not because of a contract, but because assessment-driven training takes time to show meaningful results. Most of the real progress happens in months 2 and 3. After the initial block, it's month-to-month with 30 days' written notice to cancel.

How many clients do you work with at once?

The roster is intentionally capped at 12 active clients — SF and Sacramento combined. This keeps feedback specific and response times fast. When the roster is full, there's a short waitlist. As of Q2 2026, 2 spots are available. Inquire to confirm current availability.

Is this right for me if I'm new to structured training?

Yes — in fact, this program is designed to prevent the mistakes most beginners make. The assessment catches movement issues before they become injuries. The phased programming starts at a volume your body can actually absorb. You'll build a foundation that holds up rather than a fast start that collapses.
