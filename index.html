What if I'm a complete beginner?

Beginners are who I built this for. Every program assumes you don't know what a hip hinge is, and teaches it before you do it. The exercise video library breaks down every lift step by step, "The Why" module explains the principles behind your training, and your starting volume is calibrated so you can actually recover, repeat, and progress.

If you've never touched a barbell, you're in the right place. This is the foundation I built the whole company on.

What if I have an injury or physical limitation?

NASM training specializes in corrective exercise. Your intake captures your full injury history, mobility limitations, and movement screen results, and your program is built around what your body can actually do. Not a generic template.

If your injury requires medical clearance or active rehabilitation, I'll let you know directly during intake. I'm not a substitute for physical therapy. But for working around long-standing limitations, a properly screened, individualized program is exactly what most people need.

What equipment do I need?

Whatever you have. Each program is built around your reality:

  • Full commercial gym, full barbell programming.
  • Home gym with rack and bar, the same thing scaled to your setup.
  • Dumbbells only, full hypertrophy program with smart compound substitutions.
  • Bodyweight only. Yes, even this works for beginners and early intermediates.

You specify your equipment in your intake, and I build to match. If your access changes (new gym, you start traveling) I adjust the program.

How much time do I need each week?

Most programs run 3 sessions per week, 45 to 60 minutes each. I can scale up to 4 or 5 sessions for clients with more time, or down to 2 longer sessions for clients with less. Your schedule is part of your intake. The program is built around the time you actually have, not the time you wish you had.

How is the nutrition side actually handled?

I work with a licensed nutritionist who guides every nutrition recommendation that goes into your plan. You don't talk to her directly. You talk to me. But every macro target, food list, and meal structure I give you is built on her clinical expertise, not pulled from an app or a calculator.

Foundation includes a nutrition framework with food list and meal templates. Strength and Complete include a fully personalized nutrition plan with food preferences, allergies, and lifestyle accounted for, plus ongoing adjustments as your training and body change.

How does communication actually work?

Through the coaching app and email. Foundation gets email support with a 48-hour response window. Strength gets priority chat with a 24-hour response window, plus monthly form-review video coaching. Complete adds weekly async voice or video check-ins and WhatsApp/Voxer access for quick in-the-moment questions.

Async-first communication is how I keep prices affordable. You'll never wait days for a meaningful answer. You also won't be on a Zoom call every week unless you're on Complete.

What happens after my first 12 weeks?

You keep going on a continuous monthly basis with a fully evolved program. Every 4 weeks the program updates. Every 12 weeks there's a deeper reassessment. Strength tests, movement re-screen, photo and measurement comparisons (if you opt in), and a fresh training block built on top of what you've earned.

Most clients stay 6 to 12 months. Some stay indefinitely because the program keeps evolving with them. Some leave after 6 months because they've genuinely learned how to train themselves. Which, honestly, is the win I'm working toward.

Why is this cheaper than 1-on-1 personal training?

Because the cost structure is different, not because the coaching is.

Traditional personal training is expensive because of overhead. Gym rent, equipment, drive time between clients, scheduling friction, and the simple math that one trainer can only see 5 to 8 clients a day in person. Online coaching strips all of that out. The expertise is the same. The delivery model is built for scale.

What you don't get vs. 1-on-1 in-person: a coach standing next to you while you lift. What you do get: a custom program, nutrition plan, biomechanics education, and ongoing coaching access from a credentialed coach with licensed nutritionist support behind him. For less than one in-person session per month.

Why not just YouTube? Can't I learn this for free?

YouTube gives you exercises. I give you a program. Sequenced, progressive, personalized to your body, and accountable. Free advice has no skin in your outcome. I do.

The deeper answer: free content gives you everything except the one thing you actually need. Coherence. A thousand great YouTube videos don't add up to a plan. My job is to take the principles, decide which ones apply to you, in what order, with what intensity, and with what nutrition behind them, and to adjust as your body responds.

Who is this not for?

Advanced powerlifters peaking for a meet. Bodybuilders prepping for stage. Sport-specific athletes during competitive season. People looking for shortcuts, transformation gimmicks, or quick-fix nutrition. I don't sell that.

I built this for beginners and intermediate lifters who want to train smart, eat well, and understand what they're doing. If that's you, you're in the right place. If it's not, I'd rather tell you up front than take your money for something I can't do well.
