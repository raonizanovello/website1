The Assessment

How assessment-driven actually works.

"Assessment-driven" isn't a buzzword here — it's a structured process that takes place before you do a single working set. Here's what it looks like.

01
Movement Screen

A 30-minute screen of mobility, stability, and motor control patterns. We find what needs attention before building on top of it.

02
Baseline Testing

Strength benchmarks and capacity baselines established from day one. Every future result has a clear reference point.

03
Schedule Mapping

Review of your calendar, travel patterns, gym access, and constraints. Your actual life becomes the first input to the program.

04
Program Design

A program built from scratch around your body, your schedule, and your goals. No templates — every variable is chosen for you specifically.

05
First Session

We train. We refine. You leave knowing exactly what the next 12 weeks looks like and why each choice was made.

Sample Training Week

What a typical week looks like.

Programs are built around your schedule — not the other way around. Here's a representative three-day week for a client with a standard schedule, plus the travel version.

Monday
Lower Body Foundation

Squat pattern, hip hinge, single-leg work. Loaded progressively over a 6-week block. 45–55 min total.

Wednesday
Upper Body Push / Pull

Horizontal push, vertical pull, shoulder health accessory work. Paired with anterior core. 45–55 min total.

Friday
Full Body + Conditioning

Compound movements, loaded carries, 15–20 min metabolic finisher. Total output session. 55–65 min.

Travel Week
Hotel / Minimal Equipment

Bodyweight-friendly alternatives for all three sessions. Same training intent, different tools. No skipped weeks.

Frequency, session length, and split are adjusted during the Schedule Mapping step based on what your calendar actually allows.

Client Story

From chronic back pain to a 300 lb deadlift.

A senior engineering manager came to me with a decade-old back injury, 60-hour weeks, and a history of programs that collapsed within a month. He'd been told to "just be careful" with his back and had trained around it for years.

The movement screen told a different story: limited hip mobility was the root cause — not a structural problem, a training gap. We built a three-day program he could complete during lunch or in a hotel gym.

Six months later, his back pain went from chronic to occasional. His deadlift went from 185 to 305 lbs. He trained in 46 of 52 weeks — including two international work trips.

FAQ

Common questions.

Do I need access to a specific gym?

No. Programs are designed around whatever you have access to — a full commercial gym, a home rack, hotel fitness centers, or even just a set of dumbbells. Gym access is mapped during the Schedule Mapping step and the program reflects it. If your access changes, the program changes with it.

What if I travel frequently or my schedule is unpredictable?

That's the typical use case here. Every program includes travel-week and minimal-equipment variants. If you're on the road three out of four weeks, we build the primary program around that — the stable-schedule weeks are the exception, not the rule.

Is this remote or in-person coaching?

Both options are available. Online coaching is fully remote — programming delivered through an app, check-in calls twice a month, and direct messaging access in between. In-person coaching is available in the SF Bay Area and select clients in Sacramento County. Many clients start online and add in-person sessions later.

What does a typical week actually look like?

Most clients train three days per week, 45–60 minutes per session. Sessions are structured around your schedule — not a fixed template. A standard week might be lower body, upper body, and a full-body day, with a travel alternative for each. See the Sample Training Week section above for a representative breakdown.

How does the initial assessment work?

The assessment happens in your first session. It covers a movement screen (mobility, stability, motor control), strength baselines, and a schedule mapping conversation. The full process takes about 60–75 minutes. Your first written program is delivered within 48 hours. See the Assessment section for a step-by-step breakdown.

What's the cancellation policy?

30 days' written notice to cancel. No long-term contracts — month-to-month after the initial 3-month commitment. The 3-month minimum exists because assessment-driven training takes time to show results — most meaningful progress happens in months 2 and 3.

How many clients do you work with at once?

The roster is intentionally capped at 12 active clients. This keeps feedback specific and response times fast. When the roster is full, there's a short waitlist — inquire to check current availability.
