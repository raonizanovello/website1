Common Questions

Sacramento FAQ.

Is Sacramento coaching remote or in-person?

Remote is the default and works well for most clients. Programming, check-ins, and messaging are all handled remotely. For Sacramento County clients who prefer occasional in-person sessions, that can be arranged — contact to discuss options and current availability.

Is the coaching any different from the SF practice?

No. The assessment process, programming standard, and coaching approach are identical. "Select clients in Sacramento County" is a capacity note, not a quality distinction. Sacramento clients go through the same movement screen, get the same level of programming detail, and have the same direct access between sessions.

How does the initial assessment work remotely?

Remote assessments are done over video. You'll perform a series of movement patterns while I observe. It takes about 60–75 minutes and covers mobility, stability, and motor control — the same screen as in-person. For Sacramento clients who'd prefer an in-person assessment, that can be arranged.

Do I need a gym in Sacramento?

No. Programs are designed around whatever you have access to — a commercial gym, a home rack, hotel fitness centers, or just a set of dumbbells. Equipment access is mapped during the first session and the program reflects it exactly.

How many Sacramento clients do you take on?

The total roster — SF and Sacramento combined — is capped at 12 active clients. Sacramento slots are part of that total. When the roster is full, there's a short waitlist. Inquire to check current availability.
